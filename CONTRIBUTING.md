## Step One
So, you have worked in GitHub repositories, right? That's great! We need tools and strategies to minimize the risks associated with bot spams and virus-infested code that can infiltrate our projects. Ensuring the integrity of our code is essential, and we don’t want to compromise our work with malicious content. It's crucial to implement safeguards that can automatically detect and filter out problematic submissions. By doing so, we can maintain a healthier development environment and focus on what truly matters—creating efficient and effective software solutions without the looming threat of harmful code affecting our projects and productivity.
| Requirements | ☑ Required | ‼️ Omit |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| Contributions | 30+ days older Account, Two Successful PR Merges | 1 per Month |
| Repos | Mostly Six Pins, 50 Stars | 1 Pin, 0 Stars |
| Safety Requirements | must have prior open-source experience | no open source experience |

Required means "This is accepted according to your account status." Omit means "We don't want this; it is dangerous to our repo." If your account was yesterday, it may indicate a potential issue that needs addressing. Maintaining the integrity of our system relies on accurate account information and compliance with established guidelines. Any deviations can lead to complications or risks, affecting both users and the overall functionality of services. It is essential to regularly review your account status and ensure that all details are up to date. Ignoring these aspects can prevent successful transactions and services from functioning smoothly.
